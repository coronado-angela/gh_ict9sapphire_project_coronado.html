!! how to prevent fires at home !!

"never leave your kitchen unattended whilst cooking"
 Any stove should never be left unattended. If you have to leave or are busy in another part of the house and can't watch it, turn it off. A gas stove is especially dangerous because if there's a gas leak, it can cause an explosion.

"inspect your heating sources"
 Your heating systems are a fundamental part of your home, and their regular inspections are crucial to identifying potential areas of trouble and resolving them to boost system efficiency.

"store any flammable products correctly"
 The most obvious reason for properly storing flammable liquids is to reduce the likelihood of fire. Highly flammable chemicals should be stored, transported and used in a safe manner which reduces the risk of fire and reduces the likelihood of them adding fuel to any fire on site.

"maintain/manage your wires"
 Electric currents carried by wiring, switches, plugs and appliances generate heat. Excessive or uncontrolled heat can start fires. To help prevent electrical fires, know how to use and store plugs and electrical cords.

"keep a fire extinguisher or blanket around at all times!"
 Fire extinguishers can be a small but important part of the home fire safety plan. They can save lives and property by putting out a small fire or suppressing it until the fire department arrives. Fire blankets are cheaper and more portable than fire extinguishers. They can be used to extinguish fires in hard-to-reach places. Many fire blankets can be used for persons on fire.
